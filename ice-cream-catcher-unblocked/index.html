About Ice Cream Catcher

Ice Cream Catcher is a simple reflex game built around one rule: nothing hits the floor. Pink scoops of ice cream fall from the top of the screen at random positions and increasing speeds, and your only tool is a blue paddle controlled by your mouse. Catch a scoop and your score goes up by one. Miss a single scoop and the run is over. There are no levels, no power-ups, and no second chances, which is exactly what makes chasing a high score so addictive.

How to Play

  • Move your mouse left and right to slide the paddle along the bottom of the screen.
  • Position the paddle under each falling scoop before it reaches the bottom edge.
  • Every catch adds one point to your score.
  • One missed scoop ends the game. Click the canvas to restart instantly.

Tips to Get a Higher Score

1. Park in the center, not the corners

Scoops spawn at random positions across the full width of the screen. If your paddle sits in a corner, the farthest possible scoop is a full screen-width away. From the center, the farthest scoop is only half a screen away. Return to the middle after every catch and you will rarely be caught out of position.

2. Watch the spawn point, not the ball

Each scoop falls straight down with no drift, so its landing spot is decided the moment it appears. Glance at the top of the screen, note the x-position, and move immediately. Players who stare at the ball itself react a half-second later, and at higher speeds that half-second is the difference between a catch and a game over.

3. Use small corrections late in the drop

The paddle is 70 pixels wide, so you do not need a perfect center catch. Get roughly under the scoop early, then make a small adjustment in the last moment. Overcorrecting with big mouse swings is the most common way players sail past a scoop they were already lined up for.

4. Expect overlapping drops past 20 points

Scoops spawn on a fixed timer, but their fall speeds vary. That means a slow scoop and a fast scoop can arrive near the bottom at almost the same time. When you see two scoops in the air, always prioritize the one that will land first, even if the other is closer to your paddle.
